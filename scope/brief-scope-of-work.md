## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines, in brief, the scope of **Gyan Setu** — a digital public library and knowledge
platform to be delivered for [Municipal Corporation]. It sets out the platform's objective, its
value to residents and to the Corporation, the modules and functionalities in scope, and the
boundaries of that scope.

It is a **summary scope of work** intended to establish a shared understanding of the platform's
breadth. **A detailed scope of work — covering functional specifications, technical architecture,
implementation phases, timelines, and commercials — is currently being prepared and will follow as a
separate document.**

---

## 2. Platform Objective

Every resident of [Municipal Corporation] — a school student, a competitive-exam aspirant, a
homemaker, a senior citizen, a working professional, a government employee — carries a smartphone,
but not a library. **Gyan Setu ("Knowledge Bridge") places a complete public library in every
resident's pocket and every home** — free at the point of access, available 24×7, in their own
language.

Gyan Setu consolidates capabilities that would otherwise require many separate services — a library,
a newspaper archive, an audiobook service, an exam-preparation portal, a skilling lab, and a
content-creation studio — into a single, Corporation-owned public platform.

---

## 3. Delivery Channels

The platform is delivered across two access channels:

| Channel | Description |
|---|---|
| **Web portal** | Browser-based access on any device, desktop or mobile. |
| **Mobile applications** | Native Android and iOS apps, with offline download support for low-connectivity areas. |

The platform supports **four languages — Gujarati, Hindi, English, and Sanskrit** — across its
interface and content, and is built to recognised **accessibility standards (WCAG)** so that elderly
and differently-abled residents are not left out.

## 4. What Residents Can Do — Modules & Functionalities

The platform brings together, in one place, capabilities organised into five citizen-facing pillars.
Each pillar below states the value to residents, followed by the modules and functionalities in scope.

**Platform-wide — Home & Discovery.** Every resident's entry point is a personalised **Home
Dashboard**: a unified search across the entire platform, trending and recommended content,
"continue where you left off," and simple navigation to every module — so that finding the right
book, test, or lesson takes seconds, regardless of the resident's familiarity with technology.

### 📚 Pillar 1 — Read & Discover
*Puts a vast, searchable library in every resident's hands — no queue, no distance to travel, no
closing time.*

- **Digital Library** — Searchable eBook catalogue with filters, categories, ratings, and curated
  collections across genres and languages, including regional Gujarati literature. Each title opens a
  book page with **AI-generated summaries, mind-maps, spoken (audio) summaries, and auto-generated
  quizzes** to help residents grasp and retain a book quickly.
- **eBook Reader** — In-browser reader with adjustable typography, highlights and notes,
  text-to-speech, and an **AI Reading Companion** that answers questions, explains passages, and
  translates content on demand — like a tutor beside every reader.
- **Audiobooks** — Audiobook player with playback speed control, chapter navigation, and related-title
  recommendations ("you may also like") — ideal for commuters, the visually impaired, and non-readers.
- **Newspapers & Periodicals** — Date-wise archive of newspapers, magazines, and journals, filterable
  by language, city, and date.

### 🏛️ Pillar 2 — Heritage & Knowledge Systems
*Brings the region's and India's intellectual and cultural heritage to a wide audience, and preserves
it digitally for future generations.*

- **Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS)** — Digitised manuscripts, scriptures, and folk/oral traditions,
  organised along a historical timeline (Vedic to modern era) with era-based filtering, surfacing
  local heritage such as Modhera, Adalaj, and Saurashtra's folk literature.
- **Immersive heritage experiences** — 360° virtual tours of heritage sites *(future phase)*.

### 🎓 Pillar 3 — Learn, Prepare & Skill
*Gives every aspirant coaching-grade preparation and every student modern skills — regardless of what
their family can afford.*

- **Test Preparation Hub** — Structured preparation for competitive examinations (UPSC, GPSC, SSC,
  Banking, GATE, JEE/NEET and others) with question banks, daily questions, and a personal readiness
  dashboard.
- **Mock Test Engine** — Timed, full-length examination simulation with negative marking and detailed
  score and subject-wise analysis.
- **STEM Innovation Lab** — Lessons in coding, robotics, AI, electronics, and 3D design with an
  in-browser code editor, achievement badges, leaderboards, and live workshops with booking, aligned
  to national education and innovation goals.
- **Video Library** — Curated educational video lessons organised by subject and level.

### 🎙️ Pillar 4 — Create & Contribute
*Makes the platform two-sided — local teachers and residents become creators, building a knowledge
base owned by the community.*

- **Podcast Creation Studio** — Browser-based recording, editing, and publishing of audio content
  (lessons, stories, civic awareness) by teachers, experts, and residents.
- **Podcasts Library** — Residents browse and listen to published podcast episodes, organised by
  series, through a built-in player — completing the two-sided create-and-consume loop.
- **Creator ecosystem** — A governed pathway for teachers and institutions to contribute courses,
  tests, videos, and readings, with review and moderation.

### 👤 Pillar 5 — Your Personal Space
*A personal knowledge home that remembers where each resident left off and recognises their progress.*

- **Personal Dashboard** — Per-user reading activity, progress tracking, saved bookshelf, earned
  certificates, and examination history — motivating sustained learning.
- **User accounts & access management** — Resident registration, authentication, and role-based
  access (residents, students, creators, administrators).

---

## 5. For the Corporation

Gyan Setu is not only a citizen service — it is an instrument for civic insight and governance.

- **Administration Console** — Management of content, users, creators, and platform configuration
  from one place.
- **Analytics & Insight Dashboard** — Usage trends, most-accessed content, exam-preparation
  engagement, and **ward-wise reach** — helping the Corporation understand what its residents read and
  learn, and where to invest.
- **Government integration** — Optional Aadhaar / DigiLocker-based resident verification and digital
  library membership *(future phase)*.
- **Owned and branded by the Corporation** — The platform carries the Corporation's identity, and the
  content and resident data remain with the Corporation.

---

## 6. The Possibilities — Thinking Big

Gyan Setu is designed to grow from a digital library into the civic knowledge backbone of the city.
The platform makes the following possible over time:

- **A personal AI tutor for every citizen** — adaptive learning paths, personalised recommendations,
  and doubt-solving in the resident's own language.
- **Bridging the physical and digital library** — digital membership cards and integration with
  existing public library branches and their catalogues.
- **A local knowledge economy** — residents, teachers, and local authors publishing courses,
  podcasts, and books; local heritage digitised and preserved.
- **Skilling and employment linkage** — certificates and skill tracks aligned to national education
  and skilling frameworks, connecting learners to opportunities.
- **Inclusion at the last mile** — offline-first mobile access and voice-first, low-literacy
  interfaces so that access reaches *everyone*.
- **Civic engagement** — scheme awareness, public notifications, and community learning drives run
  through the same trusted platform residents already use.
- **A replicable model** — once live for [Municipal Corporation], the same platform can be extended to
  other urban local bodies across Gujarat and India, with local branding, language, and content.

## 8. Scope Boundaries

- **In scope:** The modules and functionalities in Sections 4 and 5, delivered across the two channels
  in Section 3, in four languages, with the administration and analytics console.
- **Future phase:** Items marked *(future phase)* in Sections 4–5 and all items in Section 6, to be
  sequenced in the detailed scope of work.
- **Not covered by this document:** Functional specifications, technical architecture, data sourcing,
  implementation timelines, commercial and pricing model, and service-level commitments — all
  addressed in the detailed scope of work.
