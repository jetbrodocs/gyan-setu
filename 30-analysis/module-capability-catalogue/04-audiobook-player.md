# 04 — Audiobooks

## Purpose
Hands-free access to books for commuters, the visually impaired, non-readers, and anyone who prefers
to listen — in their own language.

---

## Full Capabilities
- **Browse & Discover** — searchable, filterable catalogue (language, subject, narrator, duration);
  curated collections; "you may also like" recommendations.
- **Player** — play/pause, 15-second skip, adjustable speed (0.75x–2x), chapter list with seek, sleep
  timer, and resume position across devices.
- **Listening tools** — bookmarks and notes at timestamps; playback queue; continue-listening.
- **Offline** — download for offline listening (subject to content rights).
- **AI & Understanding** — AI synopsis per audiobook; chapter summaries; cross-link to the eBook/text
  version of the same title.
- **Accessibility & Language** — multi-language catalogue and regional-language narration;
  visually-impaired-first design; screen-reader support.
- **Visual** — waveform visualisation during playback.
- **Community** — ratings, reviews, and sharing.

---

## Content / Data It Draws On
- Audiobook catalogue, audio files, and chapter metadata (title, author, narrator, language, duration).
- Per-resident playback position, bookmarks, and notes.
- AI synopses; links to the text versions.

## Who Uses It
- **Every resident**, especially **visually-impaired residents, seniors, commuters, and non-readers**.

---

## Notes for the Prioritisation Pass (later — not decisions)
- Streaming playback, speed, chapters, and recommendations exist in the demo.
- Multi-language catalogue growth, offline download, resume-position persistence, AI synopsis, and
  reducing the third-party streaming dependency are the main build-out.
