# 03 — eBook Reader

## Purpose
A comfortable, accessible reading experience for any book — with an AI companion that helps every
resident understand what they read, in their own language.

---

## Full Capabilities
- **Reading experience** — chapter-by-chapter table of contents; adjustable font size, typeface,
  spacing, and themes (light/dark/sepia); page or scroll view; full-screen distraction-free mode.
- **Progress & sync** — automatic progress tracking (page and %); resume exactly where left off from
  any device; reading-time estimates.
- **Annotation** — highlights, bookmarks, and personal notes that persist and are searchable within a
  book; export or share notes.
- **AI Reading Companion** — ask questions about the current chapter, explain passages, summarise,
  define words, and discuss themes — grounded in the book's own text.
- **Translate & language** — translate passages or whole chapters on demand; read in the resident's
  language.
- **Read-aloud (TTS)** — natural text-to-speech narration with speed control and follow-along
  highlighting.
- **Study tools** — jump to the book's AI summary, mind-map, and quiz; generate flashcards; cite and
  quote passages.
- **Accessibility** — large-print, high-contrast, and dyslexia-friendly options; screen-reader and
  keyboard navigation; WCAG compliance.
- **Light social** — share a quote; optionally see popular (moderated) highlights.

---

## Content / Data It Draws On
- The full text of each book.
- Per-resident highlights, notes, bookmarks, and reading progress.
- AI Reading Companion context; text-to-speech audio; translations.

## Who Uses It
- **Every resident** reading a book; **students** using the study tools; **seniors and
  differently-abled residents** relying on accessibility features.

---

## Notes for the Prioritisation Pass (later — not decisions)
- Reader, AI Companion, and progress tracking exist in the demo.
- Persistent annotations, TTS, on-demand translation, study tools, and accessibility depth are the
  main build-out.
