# 05 — Newspapers & Periodicals

## Purpose
A daily and archival reading room of newspapers, magazines, and journals — across languages and
cities — open to every resident for free.

---

## Full Capabilities
- **Sections** — Newspapers, Magazines, and Journals as distinct, browsable sections.
- **Browse & Filter** — by language, city/edition, publication, and date via a calendar picker; a
  deep archive of past editions.
- **Full-page Reader** — realistic full-page layout with zoom, page navigation, and single-article
  view; clip and save articles.
- **Search** — across editions and archives by keyword, publication, or date, including full-text
  article search.
- **Personalisation** — follow favourite publications; a daily digest; new-edition notifications;
  saved/clipped articles; reading history.
- **AI & Understanding** — article summaries; translate articles; topic tracking; a "today in brief."
- **Accessibility & Language** — reader/reflow mode; read-aloud (TTS); large-print; multi-language.
- **Civic Content** — Corporation bulletins, official gazettes and notices, and community newsletters.
- **Content Operations (behind the scenes)** — ingestion of editions from publishers; rights and
  licensing; archive management.

---

## Content / Data It Draws On
- Newspaper, magazine, and journal editions (page images and article text) with language, city, date,
  and publication metadata.
- Per-resident follows, clips, and reading history.
- AI summaries and translations.

## Who Uses It
- **Every resident** — especially **seniors**, **exam aspirants** (current affairs), and general
  readers; **the Corporation** for official notices.

---

## Notes for the Prioritisation Pass (later — not decisions)
- The demo has date/language/city archive browsing and a full-page view.
- Magazines/journals sections, real publisher ingestion, full-text search, follows/digests, AI
  summaries/translation, and official gazettes are the main build-out. Content sourcing/licensing is a
  partnerships dependency.
